# Tech Overview

This section covers the Tech section.  

Here’s a brief overview of modern “tech”:

**Computers** are the physical backbone of digital technology. From early mainframes to today’s multi-core personal devices, enterprise servers, and specialized AI accelerators, computers execute computations using processors (CPUs/GPUs), memory, and storage. Miniaturization, energy efficiency, and architectural shifts (like ARM-based chips and chiplet designs) have made computing power ubiquitous across industries.

**Peripherals** are external devices that extend a computer’s capabilities. Traditional input/output tools (keyboards, mice, monitors, printers) have evolved into high-speed, often wireless interfaces like USB-C/Thunderbolt docks, AR/VR headsets, biometric scanners, and smart IoT sensors. Modern peripherals prioritize seamless connectivity, cross-platform compatibility, and plug-and-play functionality, bridging human intent and machine execution.

**Silicon Valley** is the historic and ongoing epicenter of global tech innovation. Centered in California’s Santa Clara Valley, it earned its name from the semiconductor industry but grew into a dense ecosystem of startups, venture capital, research institutions, and tech giants. Its culture of rapid prototyping, risk tolerance, and talent concentration has repeatedly catalyzed paradigm shifts (personal computing, internet, mobile, cloud, AI), though it also navigates ongoing debates around regulation, workforce equity, and sustainable growth.

**Software** is the intangible instruction layer that makes hardware useful. It spans operating systems, firmware, applications, cloud platforms, and AI/ML models. Development practices have shifted toward agile, open-source, and DevOps workflows, while delivery models emphasize SaaS, APIs, and continuous updates. Software now drives differentiation more than hardware alone, enabling automation, data analytics, and intelligent user experiences.

**Together**, these components form a tightly integrated ecosystem: computers provide the computational foundation, peripherals enable interaction and data capture, software delivers functionality and intelligence, and Silicon Valley’s innovation engine accelerates their co-evolution. Current trajectories point toward AI-native computing, edge processing, unified hardware-software design, and sustainability-driven engineering, keeping the tech landscape dynamic and deeply embedded in modern society.
